What Makes Tommy Different

The Fleetwood Swing DNA

🤲

Strong Grip

Right hand noticeably under the club. Promotes a draw and helps control the face through impact.

🔄

Full Chest Turn

Back fully facing the target at the top. One of the widest shoulder turns on tour for his height.

↙️

Deep Shallowing

Right shoulder drops low on the way down. The "underhanded javelin" move that creates tremendous lag and power.

✋

Flat Lead Wrist

🎯

Short Follow-Through

🧘

Quiet Lower Body

Minimal sway on the backswing. Stable base allows the upper body to dominate the turn.

The Training Programme

The 8 Essential Drills

1

Set Up & Grip

Build Tommy's foundation before touching anything else

The Strong Grip Check
Setup Daily

Why Tommy does it

Tommy's strong right-hand grip is the engine behind his consistent draw and face control. Without this, the other moves won't produce the same results.

  1. Address the ball normally, then look down at your left hand — you should see 2.5 to 3 knuckles.
  2. Place the right hand so the palm faces slightly downward (under the club, not to the side). The V formed by your thumb and index should point to your right shoulder.
  3. Hold a club at home and check in a mirror — the right forearm should look slightly more under the shaft than feels natural at first.
  4. Hit 10 chip shots to cement the feeling before any full swings.
The Feel"My right hand feels like it's more under the grip than I'm used to. Like I'm carrying a tray."
Wide Stance & Spine Tilt
Setup Mirror

Why Tommy does it

Tommy sets up with a slightly wider than average stance and tilts away from the target at address. This biased setup supports his big shoulder turn and shallow downswing.

  1. Stand in front of a mirror. Set up with feet slightly wider than shoulder width for irons, wider still for driver.
  2. Feel your weight fractionally favouring the right side (60/40). Right shoulder should sit noticeably lower than the left.
  3. Ball position just inside the lead heel for irons. Check you're balanced in the middle of your feet — not on your toes or heels.
  4. Relax your upper back (thoracic spine). Tommy keeps a soft, relaxed posture at address — no tension in the shoulders.
The Feel"I'm sitting into it slightly, weight a touch back. Right shoulder down. Relaxed and ready."
2

Takeaway & Backswing

Chest turn and club staying in front of the body

Chest Turn Takeaway Drill
Backswing Mirror

Why Tommy does it

Tommy initiates the swing by turning his chest away from the target — not by pushing with his hands. The club stays connected in front of his body throughout the takeaway.

  1. Hold a headcover under your lead armpit. Make a takeaway — if it drops, your arms are disconnecting from your body rotation.
  2. Turn until you can see your left chest logo (or left pec) in the mirror. That's where the takeaway ends and the full turn begins.
  3. Hips should rotate freely alongside the shoulders — don't restrict them. Tommy doesn't hold his hips back.
  4. Do this slowly 20 times, stopping at halfway back to check the club shaft is on the correct plane (parallel to the target line).
The Feel"I'm turning my chest, not lifting my arms. The club is just along for the ride."
Full Shoulder Turn & Flat Wrist Drill
Backswing Key Move

Why Tommy does it

Tommy's back fully faces the target at the top, with a flat or slightly bowed lead wrist and the clubface slightly closed. This is the most distinctive and difficult part of his swing to copy.

  1. At address, place an alignment stick across your shoulders. On the backswing, turn until the stick points at the target — that's a full shoulder turn.
  2. At the top, check your lead wrist in a mirror — it should be flat or very slightly bowed (cupped means the face is open, which is NOT Fleetwood).
  3. To train the bowed wrist: use a glove under your lead wrist at the top, or simply practise the top position 30 times at home with a club, checking the wrist each rep.
  4. The right forearm should be more under the club at the top — not over it. This is the "delivery position" Tommy is famous for.
The Feel"Full turn, back to the target. Left wrist feels firm and flat, not cupped. Right forearm under the shaft."
3

Transition & Downswing

The shallowing move — Tommy's secret weapon

Drop & Shallow Drill (Pump Drill)
Advanced Downswing

Why Tommy does it

Tommy's downswing starts with a lateral shift and a drop of the right shoulder — incredibly low. This keeps him in the "underhanded javelin throw" slot and is the source of his power and consistency from any lie.

  1. Go to the top of your backswing. Stop. Now without swinging, just drop your right shoulder as low as it can go while shifting your hips laterally toward the target. Feel the club shallow behind you.
  2. Repeat this "pump" movement 5 times before each swing during practice. Stop at halfway down and check the shaft is shallower than it was at the top.
  3. Feel your right elbow tucking close to your right hip as you drop. This is the slot.
  4. Hit punch shots at 60% speed focusing only on this transition — don't worry about the finish.
The Feel"Right shoulder drops down toward the ball, not out toward the target. I'm throwing underarm, not overarm."
4

Impact

Right forearm under, trail arm bent, centre contact

Impact Bag / Towel Under Armpit Drill
Impact At Home

Why Tommy does it

Tommy keeps his right forearm well under the left at impact and maintains a bent trail arm. This prevents the flip that causes hooks and inconsistency.

  1. Set up an impact bag (or a heavy cushion). Strike it and hold the impact position. Check: right forearm should be under the left, both forearms rotating together.
  2. Alternatively, use a towel under the right armpit. If it falls during impact, your right arm is disconnecting and flipping.
  3. Hit 20 slow-motion chip shots focusing purely on the forearm position through the ball.
  4. Progress to half swings and then full swings once the feel is embedded.
The Feel"Right forearm rotating under the left. Feeling like both arms are working together through the ball — not one flipping past the other."
5

Follow-Through

Tommy's most signature and copied move

Punch Shot Follow-Through Drill
Signature Follow-Through

Why Tommy does it

Tommy's abbreviated follow-through is no accident — he built it deliberately through thousands of punch shots. It keeps his upper body centred over the ball and stops him from flipping or spinning out. The short finish is actually a sign of great impact, not a restriction.

  1. Hit punch shots with a 7-iron — aim to finish with the club shaft no higher than waist height, hands leading, weight fully transferred.
  2. Focus on the zone from "club parallel before impact" to "club parallel after impact." That's Tommy's impact zone. Dial this in first.
  3. Gradually allow the follow-through to extend naturally — but keep the feeling of the upper body staying on top of the ball, clearing the left hip to drive the club around rather than reaching up.
  4. Hit 30 punch shots per session until the abbreviated finish feels completely natural. Then transfer it to full swings.
The Feel"Punching through. Body over the ball. Left hip clearing but upper body not lunging. Finish feels compact, not reaching."
Alignment Stick Gate Drill
Path Range

Why Tommy does it

Tommy uses alignment sticks to prevent his downswing getting too far in-to-out. This keeps his path neutral and produces that controlled, penetrating ball flight he's known for.

  1. Place two alignment sticks on the ground just outside the ball, angled to create a gate slightly wider than your clubhead.
  2. The sticks are there to stop the downswing getting too far in-to-out (which would produce a block or snap hook).
  3. Swing through the gate — you should feel a slightly more neutral path than an exaggerated inside attack.
  4. Pair with the punch shot drill above for maximum effect.
The Feel"Swinging through the gate. Not too in-to-out. Path feels more neutral than my natural tendencies."
3+

Sequencing & Strike Control

The missing ingredients behind Fleetwood's ball striking

Step-Through Pressure Shift Drill
Sequencing Essential

Why Tommy does it

Fleetwood shifts pressure into his lead side early. This prevents getting stuck underneath and allows his famous shallow move to work correctly.

  1. Set up normally with a mid iron.
  2. Start the downswing and step toward the target with your lead foot.
  3. Allow the body to continue rotating through impact.
  4. Hit 15–20 half shots focusing on pressure moving left before the club releases.
The Feel"Pressure moves left first, then the club shallows."
Low Point Line Drill
Contact Range

Why Tommy does it

Elite ball striking comes from controlling low point. Fleetwood's consistency is built on predictable turf interaction.

  1. Draw a line on the turf or use spray paint on a practice area.
  2. Place the ball just ahead of the line.
  3. Strike shots with the divot beginning on or just after the line.
  4. Continue until contact becomes predictable and compressed.
The Feel"Ball first. Turf second. Divot after the ball."
3:1 Tempo Drill
Tempo Rhythm

Why Tommy does it

Fleetwood's transition is smooth and unhurried. Tempo allows the shallowing move to happen naturally.

  1. Count "one-two-three" on the backswing.
  2. Count "one" through the downswing and impact.
  3. Maintain the same rhythm regardless of club.
  4. Hit 20 balls focusing only on timing and balance.
The Feel"Smooth away, athletic through."
Face Control Ladder
Shot Shaping Control

Why Tommy does it

Fleetwood's real superpower is face control. Learning to vary ball flight builds awareness and consistency.

  1. Hit 5 fades.
  2. Hit 5 straight shots.
  3. Hit 5 draws.
  4. Only make small face adjustments while keeping the same swing.
The Feel"Same swing. Different face."
